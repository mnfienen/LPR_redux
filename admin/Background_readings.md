## Initial Readings for Project 4
    Use these to gain insight into the range of water users in the area.
    Also use them to develop a conceptual model of the hydrogeologic and hydromet systems.
    Focus on background and concepts, not development of models of the LPR.
    
[Water: Essays for the Central Sands](https://agwater.extension.wisc.edu/articles/walking-on-water-essays-for-the-central-sands/)

    Required 'group read'.  Please set up a shared document where you can sign up to read one essay.
        Be prepared to bring this perspective to group and class-wide conversations about water 'use'.

[A Historic Pumping Test Movie](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZNJcVQBIE0Y)

    A priceless look into the history of our science ... worth watching!
        Best watched with popcorn and a soda ... or a Bud and a Marlboro ... your choice.

[Annotated bibliography on the Central Sands](https://data.wgnhs.wisc.edu/pubshare/WOFR2018-04.pdf)
    
    Compiled by WDNR and Wisconsin Geolog and Nat History Surv for their Central Sands Lakes Study. 

[Weeks and Strangeland 1971](https://pubs.usgs.gov/publication/ofr70362)

    A seminal works on irrigation and streamflow in the area.
    
[Kniffen et al, 2014](https://learningstore.extension.wisc.edu/products/sustaining-central-sands-water-resources-p1800) 

    A review paper from UW-Extension intended to be for more of common framework for a lay-audience. 
      Note the download link isn't working at the moment, we may have to upload the PDF to D2L

[Bradbury et al 2017](https://wgnhs.wisc.edu/catalog/publication/000946/resource/b111)

    This is the report on the LPR model.  Focus on the background and methods, not model development.  

[Appendix F of the Central Sands Lakes Study](https://widnr.widen.net/view/pdf/z8j5lsfp00/DG_CSLSAppendixF_2021.pdf)

    A history of Land Use and irrigation in the Central Sands.
